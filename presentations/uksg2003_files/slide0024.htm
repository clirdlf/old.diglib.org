An illustrative (and unsustainable) problem
oA faculty member who is building a reading list on a class web page often takes the paper copy of a journal – or asks their e-reserves operation to do the same – and scans in the relevant article even if that material is licensed online.  Why?  Because when he or she goes to a site they are so frustrated by the inability to link at a sufficiently granular level that going back to the paper copy is quicker.
o
oPoor interface between library content management systems and courseware; little ability to link from course page to the article level in licensed content
o
oThis is not a technology problem – it is one of habit, branding, ownership, perceived value, misguided competition, unarticulated needs.
