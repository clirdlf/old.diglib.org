Data silos: So what’s the problem?
oEvery publisher is an island; they produce silos of data that plays badly with others – little ability to work cross-publisher and cross-aggregator.  They build product that can only appear on their terms, in their interface.  Leads to passive use.
o
oLibraries don’t shelve by publisher and users don’t work this way – runs counter to our normal patterns of behavior.
o
oLibraries face a chronic inability to repackage content, especially journals, for local use – we are failing in our service mission to our customers
o
oEndless different interfaces; users face time-consuming searches in and out of vendors’ web pages to find material
