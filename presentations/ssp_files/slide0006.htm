Looking Back
nLet’s glimpse back into our dark primordial past as purveyors and consumers of digital content: nIn the early 1990s, academic libraries persuaded publishers of early digital library content to provide that material on tape for local loading or online, rather than on CD-Rom disks.  Why? nMinor reason: solved by online access from a publisher’s site: it was inconvenient to have to go to a physical building to use a digital library item, which was capable of traveling to you across a network.
