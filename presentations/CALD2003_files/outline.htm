Digital Library Federation
  • Thirty partners – US academic libraries ; four allies (RLG; OCLC; CNI; Los Alamos)
  • Created in 1995 by directors of US research libraries
  • Fills a need not simply met by large library organizations : focuses exclusively on digital library needs and strategies; designed to be nimble, agile, and collaborative
  • Practical and strategic areas of activity
3
Copyright
  • Leave it to someone else .… Except to say :
    • Be diligent but not paranoid
    • Be generally informed and activist
    • Protect your own – ban “public domain” from your website unless you really mean to give up all control over re-use and re-sale.  Typically, you mean “publicly accessible”.


  • Watch out for false assumptions :
    • “It’s in copyright, we can’t digitize it” – it simply means you need to obtain permission
    • “The publisher said ‘no’ (or nothing) – but are they the rights holder?  Toscini versus NYT and Rosetta Books versus Random House are cautionary tales in this regard.
4
Digitization
  • Standards rule: digitizing is expensive enough to do and the results are difficult to preserve, so you better get it mostly right the first time.


  • Standards rock: archival-quality digital material is nimble, malleable, flexible, and permanent – it can be shaped to users’ evolving needs and therefore much more likely to be successful in the academic arena (donors like to see that what they funded is enduring and successful)

Fundraising in the Digital Library; or, “When you come to a fork in the road, take it.” (Yogi Berra)
  • Is fundraising for the digital library fundamentally different than fundraising for the analog library – are we at a fork in the road?
  • Same core skills needed – clarity, persuasion, sincerity, articulation of value and excitement, building and maintaining relationships, football game attendance.
7
Grants and Gifts -- Institutions
  • Wider range of institutions – NSF; NIH; Mellon; NEH; FIPSE; IMLS; state funding;
  • Messier picture now – lots of different rules, lots of shifting priorities
  • But – reflection that in its digital form, the academic library content is potentially more useful and accessible to a wider set of audiences (high school; general public; non-US; college; physically challenged)

Grants and gifts -- Individual donors
  • Easier to tailor area of donor-funded activity to their personal interests
  • Easier to show them the product at long distance – to maintain the relationship
  • Easier for them to show others what their largesse has produced
  • Easier to demonstrate use
