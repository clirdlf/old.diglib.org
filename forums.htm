DLF Forums convene twice a year and includes digital library practitioners from member institutions. It serves as a meeting place, a market place, and a congress. As a meeting place it provides an opportunity for DLF initiatives to conduct their business and to present their work to the broader membership. As a market place, it provides an opportunity for practitioners to share experiences and practices with one another and to support a broad level of information-sharing between professional staff. As a congress, it provides an opportunity for the DLF to review and assess its programs and its progress with input from the broader membership community.

DLF Spring Forum 2002
The DLF Spring Forum 2002 was held in Chicago at the Wyndham Chicago, on May 10–12, 2002. Papers and panel discussions dealt with all aspects of the digital library. At this Forum, there was a special emphasis on interoperability—what it is, why it is significant for research libraries and their patrons, and how it may be achieved. The topic was be approached from all its many technological, organizational, definitional, political and other aspects. The Spring Forum 2002 also provided an opportunity to showcase a number of DLF initiatives and, as a new departure, to convene birds of a feather (BOF) discussions on topics offered by participants.
DLF Fall Forum 2001
The DLF Fall Forum 2001 was held in Pittsburgh at the Marriott City Center, November 16–18, 2001. As in previous Forums, papers and panel discussions dealt with all aspects of digital library development, for example: architectural and technical issues; digital collections; digital preservation; digital library standards and practices; and organizational, policy, and business issues. The Fall Forum 2001 also provided an opportunity to showcase a number of DLF initiatives.
DLF Spring Forum 2001
The DLF Spring Forum 2001 was held in San Francisco at the Hyatt Fisherman's Wharf Hotel on May 4–6, 2001. Papers and panel discussions dealt with all aspects of digital library development, for example: architectural and technical issues; digital collections; digital preservation; digital library standards and practices; and organizational, policy, and business issues. Particular emphasis was given to papers and panel discussions that addressed issues having to do with digital library use, users, and user support. The Spring Forum 2001 also provided an opportunity to showcase a number of DLF initiatives. In addition, a special pre-Forum workshop was held on the morning of May 4, 2001 on methods for assessing digital library use.
DLF Fall Forum 2000
The Fall Forum 2000 was held on November 18–20, 2000 in Chicago, Illinois. Presentations and panel discussions brought a variety of perspectives to bear on the development, maintenance, and use of high-quality digital collections and reflect upon how these activities affect the library's organization and its institutional roles. See a description of the Forum, the official summary, the papers & agenda, and a participant list.
DLF Forum on Digital Library Organizations, Spring 2000
The Spring Forum 2000 provided an opportunity for members to exchange experiences of the organization, management, and funding of digital library initiatives. See the papers presented at the Forum, its agenda, and a participant list.
DLF Forum on Digital Library Practices, Summer 1999
With a decidedly technical focus, papers presented at the Summer Forum 1999 evaluated experience with digital library architectures, systems and tools. See a description of the Forum, the agenda, and a participant list.
