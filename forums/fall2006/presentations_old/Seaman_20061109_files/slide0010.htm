Following session at spring 2005 DLF Forum, where we introduced documents and plans for these workshops and gathered feedback on what was needed, we conducted 2 separate workshops for institutions considering OAI implementation:

DLF OAI Implementers Workshops
Fall 2005 at DLF Forum
August 2006 at Stanford

Both workshops requested 2 reps from institution, preferably cataloger/metadata librarian & techie familiar with infrastructure demands. The second workshop built on the lessons of the first, and was by many accounts successful in its ability to wed overviews and instruction with hands-on trouble-shooting and discussion. By interspersing overview presentations with topically focused, small group workshops (participants chose for themselves which to attend, could stay in the same focused workgroup throughout or switch to a new workgroup the next time around), kept all participants engaged, up-to-speed on discussions, and addressed specific questions and institutional challenges. Even those institutions not far enough along in OAI implementation to have specific needs said that they benefitted from sitting in on discussion.

TRAINING MATERIALS
-Quick list of resources, referenced during presentations and one-on-one workshops
-Supplemented presentation information, giving participants an alternative source of info to follow and a quick answer to more basic questions
-Standalone, so can be re-referenced by participants following workshop
-Can be re-used by institutions for local workshops

LOOKING FORWARD
-especially at the last workshop, participants sought more workshops like this to support OAI implementation:
-Local Workshops (just for digital-library division or OAI-implementation team + administrators)
-Consultation (on-site assistance with very specific implementation issues: technical infrastructure, metadata training)
