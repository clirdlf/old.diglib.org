Clear and concise information
I developed and edited the core documents, along with Martin Halbert and Katherine Skinner at Emory University, to ensure that they were sufficiently informative but concise. Other members of the project team reviewed the documents and suggested content (especially Kat Hagedorn and Tom Habing); Sarah Shreeves contributed significantly to the metadata “best practices” and MODS content.
STANDALONE, but part of SERIES
(in addition to one handout summarizing the IMLS project) 6 documents that address different training & information needs of DLF institutions
-OAI Implementation: Administrative Planning - guidelines for budgeting resources for and timeing an OAI implementation
-Summary of Metadata Best Practices - rationale and tips for creating shareable metadata
-Summary of the DLF Aquifer MODS Profile - summary of the guidelines (updating now to reflect recently released MODS guidelines)
-OAI Tools -software, scripts, and systems that data & service providers can use to create, harvest, and modify OAI records
-OAI “Cheat Sheet”: Rapid Deployment Strategies - how to quickly implement using existing technical infrastructure
-The Case for OAI - rationale for implementing OA
