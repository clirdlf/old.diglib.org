Sustainability
lHow will we ensure that this resource continues to be used and valued by researchers?
¡Scholars’ active participation at all levels & phases of project development
¡Regular consultations with scholars to discuss design & development issues
l
COLLABORATION
-scholars are vested in the content of this resource, committed to seeing it continue to grow and expanding its audience
-Digital librarians are vested in the longevity of this resource, committed to finding ways to maintain it and preserve it at minimal effort & expense

Helps ensure the continued strength and value of the resource
Participants feel ownership of the project, invested in project
Thoughtful work and suggestions help project team anticipate and prepare for future use and development of resource
The more committed partners involved, the less likely a few individuals and institutions will bear the load of maintaining the resource

What this looks like on the ground:
PARTICIPATION
Steering Committee (data cleaning, creating editorial review system and digital preservation network, feedback on database functionality & interface design)
- includes original authors of the CD-ROM, who contributed much of the original data
Advisory Board (development of curricular & auxiliary materials for K-12 audiences, feedback on database functionality & interface design)
-includes researchers who contributed data to original database
Visiting Professors/Research Associates (data gathering, data cleaning, feedback on database functionality & interface design
-includes individuals who contributed data to original database and also those responsible for helping estimate the total volume of the slave trade

CONSULTATION
- every week, side-by-side discussion with programmers, GIS consultant, and/or web-design consultant as questions occur during development (ex: mapping display issues, navigating website, search interface design, tweaking algorithms for estimating total volume of slave trade, etc.)
