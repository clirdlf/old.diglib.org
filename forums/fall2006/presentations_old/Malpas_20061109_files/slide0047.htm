Urgent Question (4)
§As a contributor to the mass digitization enterprise, do you expect other institutions or other entities to be able to build collections or services on the materials you've contributed?
§
§Related user-contributed question:
§Do we imagine a gigantic corpus of text (and images and sound), to be analyzed and explored as a unity? Or a collection of bibliographic/visual/sonic "atoms," each discrete and separately discoverable? Or intermediate aggregations built around particular subjects or other concerns?
§
Sian – how is the technical steering committee for Alouette addressing this concern?  Does the architecture effectively support re-use of contributed collections?  How does it compare to the architecture of the OCA collections?
Stuart – the Loughborough study concluded that the UK digitization environment is fundamentally fragmented:  how will the new UK e-services framework “make it whole”?  Who (if anyone) is responsible for ensuring that national investments in content conversion continue to bear fruit years from now?
Robin – there is so far little evidence that UC will develop a separate discovery/use environment for its Google or OCA contributions – do you anticipate that a third party will step in to provide such services?  In the case of the Google collections, will it even be possible (have you retained the rights to this kind of re-use?)
Barbara – you’ve spoken in other contexts about the need for “format-blind,” thematic aggregations of content.  What steps has NYPL taken to ensure that it’s digital collections can support this kind of use?  NYPL Performing Arts is now accessioning personal and archival collections in born-digital formats – are rights to re-used negotiated as part of the deed of gift?  What work remains to be done to bring collections specialists and selectors into digital library planning?
