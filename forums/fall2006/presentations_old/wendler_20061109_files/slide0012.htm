So how is this relevant today? Harvard’s source metadata may be decidedly shabby and old-school at this point, but aspects of the record structure are in some ways forebears of the very newest standards for visual resource metadata: VRA Core Categories 4.0 and the Cataloging Cultural Objects descriptive guidelines. VRA 4.0, which is still not formally released, and CCO, published last summer, both define three record types: Collection, Work, and Image. CCO goes so far as to state “Records for individual works or images can be hierarchically linked as part of the group, collection, or series record.”  Harvard’s groups, works, and surrogates are not so different from the VRA and CCO record type concepts of collection, work, and image.

The key differences are that 1) in VIA records related descriptions are encapsulated in a single record and 2) the relationships among descriptions are loosely rather than strictly typed.
