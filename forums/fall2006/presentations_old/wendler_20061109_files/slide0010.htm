Choosing an individual component gets you to the entire branch of metadata for that item.

I’ve consciously talked about VIA record structure rather than data model, because on Harvard’s side, this was a very pragmatic way for catalogers to apply an organization to a set of materials. The structure is flexible but consciously limited:

It does not rigorously type relationships among objects or images. For example, images of a whole work and images of details are siblings in our records: they are all just representations of the work. In cases like Fallingwater, librarians chose to create multiple records applying an organization to the materials.
It is arbitrarily limited to three tiers (groups of works, individual works, surrogates). Links to digital images can occur at any of the three levels.

It is arbitrarily limited to 300 children per parent.
