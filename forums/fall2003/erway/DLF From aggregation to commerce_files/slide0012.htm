We’ve addressed many challenges:
--In addition to supporting digital representations of images, documents, audio files, video clips, we have implemented a METS viewer to allow for navigation of complex digital objects.
--In aggregating the records created by diverse descriptive practices, we’ve combined different formats, different approaches, different vocabularies, different levels of cataloging, and very thick and very thin records.  The greatest amount of effort is spent analyzing each collection, mapping it to our scheme, and doing some targeted normalization.
--Meaningful integration across collections required identifying common access points, as well as making record displays and surrogate viewing as consistent as possible.
--Building a reliable infrastructure was a real challenge because we were working in a brand new environment, learning new skills as we went:
IBM DB2 and WebSphere,
writing routines (with colorful names like CultureVulture and FotoRooter) to harvest images and create derivatives.
establishing a Hierarchical Storage Management system and a new authentication server
--In order for the service to be useful in academia, it was important to have consistent terms and conditions.  The Policy Advisory Group led the Alliance in coming up with agreements as to institutional rights and responsibilities
--And the biggest ongoing challenge is content development.  As you know, institutions are pulled in many directions.  Even though we make it as easy as possible to contribute collections, it takes an army of nice, but needling, RLG staff and a bunch of responsive and engaged member staff to keep the resource growing.
