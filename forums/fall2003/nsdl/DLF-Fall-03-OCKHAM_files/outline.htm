The National Science Digital Library (NSDL)
  • The NSDL was established by the National Science Foundation (NSF) to “catalyze and support continual improvement in the quality of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) education”
  • Envisioned as one of the most comprehensive digital educational networks in the world

  • Targeted Research

NSDL & Research Libraries
  • Noticeable lack of engagement between the established research library community and NSDL
  • As part of sustainability exploration, the NSDL is looking for better connections with librarians
  • The OCKHAM and CDL projects funded by NSDL this year reflect this
17
OCKHAM Background
  • Grew out of a DLF meeting in October 2001 discussing open source software standards
  • DLF subsequently sponsored a series of meetings by the OCKHAM working group in 2002 and 2003 to analyze commonalities in digital library architectures and possibilities for future interoperability between DL systems
18
Core Problems Studied
  • Interoperability
    • -- Difficulty integrating monolithic systems
    • -- Need for modularity of DL services & migratability of DL content
  • Affordability
    • -- How to achieve sustainability through group efforts
    • -- Cost of DL infrastructures, in terms of both purchase and development costs
19
OCKHAM Discussions
  • Meetings in Atlanta & ECDL of: digital librarians, vendors, as well as DL/CS researchers
  • Compared prominent DL architectures, standards/protocols, and reference models
  • Cultivated idea of lightweight protocol reference models
  • Proposed catalytic project focused on NSDL

OCKHAM NSDL Project
  • NSDL has assembled an extraordinary set of resources
  • Yet, there has been limited integration and deployment of NSDL through the existing library community
  • Opportunity exists to explore mechanisms to facilitate dissemination and integration of NSDL resources through existing library service channels

Key Design Features
  • P2P network of interoperable web services using SOAP, WSDL, UDDI protocols
  • Hope to create an extensible framework that will make it easy for other institutions to both connect to the network and also contribute to it in the future
