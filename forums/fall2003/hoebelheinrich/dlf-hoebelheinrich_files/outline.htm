Underlying Assumptions:  Metadata
  • Require at least a minimal, common set of MD elements across repository collections to achieve just enough MD
  • Design for preservation focused digital repository
  • Strongly recommend more MD depending upon risk factors associated with digital resource(s)
  • Leverage existing MD whenever possible
  • Most important to allow searchers to locate & look at digital resource rather than describe
  • Use of METS for SIP, AIP, DIP into / out of SDR
7
Underlying Assumptions:  Search & Retrieval
  • Use of meta-searching tool for initial searching across collections (Sirsis SingleSearch integrated with OPAC & SDR
  • Allow for more advanced searching deeper into specific collections, if MD and other search engines exist, e.g., Luna
8
Underlying Assumptions:  Workflow
  • Facilitate workflow efficiency
  • Distributed digitization, MD creation & MD enhancement environments
  • Strongly encourage use of general, but customizable (to some extent)  MS SQL database & web GUI for MD creation & automatic ingestion into SDR
  • Use automated processes to the extent possible & feasible (duh!)

So whats just enough MD?
  • Descriptive Conflicts:
    • Structured MD and keyword searching
      • Food & Agriculture Organization of the UN study on AGRIS database = Google works with structured MD
      • SUL mini-study of effectiveness of MARC EL3 records = keyword works as well as subject terms particularly if names are used, use singular terms not plural, use subj phrases under authority control, specific not general term

So whats just enough MD?
    • Narrowly focused element requirements to find, identify and locate - - Got OpenURL?
    • To facilitate selection:  target full text searching tools on unstructured, fielded MD (e.g., Title/Description or Creator/Contributor)
    • Creative indexing
    • Collaborate & leverage data control where possible

So, what does it mean to have a preservation repository anyway?
  • In terms of MD, were not yet sure, but soon
    • RLG / OCLC Preservation Workgroups
      • Framework for Preservation MD
      • PREmis:  core Preservation MD element set & implementation strategies
    • Much work being done in UK, NZ, AU
    • SUL poised to incorporate into workflow the assessment of levels of risk associated with collections / object types
      • SUL DRAFT questionnaire based partly on analysis done by William G. LeFurgy on levels of service associated with digital repositories & on OAIS reference model analysis of whats necessary for SIPs, AIPs, and DIPs.
