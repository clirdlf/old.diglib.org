Digital Video Basics
A video signal consists of luminance and chrominance information
Luminance – brightness, varying from white to black (abbreviated as Y)
Chrominance – color (hue & saturation), conveyed as a pair of color difference signals:
nR-Y (hue & saturation for red, without luminance)
nB-Y (hue & saturation for blue, without luminance)
Historically, this allows for you to transmit a B&W TV image with the same signal used for color TV.
B&W sets just process the luminance information; color TV processes it all.
