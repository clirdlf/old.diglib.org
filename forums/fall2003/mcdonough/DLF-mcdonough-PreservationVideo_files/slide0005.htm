Digital Video Basics
Why not 4:4:4 sampling?
n720 x 486 resolution = 349,920 pixels per frame
n349,920 pixels x 10 bits/sample x 3 samples/pixel = 10,497,600 bits per frame
n10,497,600 bits/frame X 29.97 frames/second = 314,613,072 bits per second
n314,613,072 bps x 3600 seconds = ~141.58 GB/hour
nFor 1920x1080 HDTV, more like 840 GB/hour
4:2:2 sampling drops that rate by a third with almost no perceptible difference in quality.  4:2:0 and 4:1:1 drop it in half.
Lesson: uncompressed, full resolution digital video is very storage intensive.  Subsampling is basically a form of lossy compression.
