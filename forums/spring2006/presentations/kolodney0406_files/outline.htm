Context
  • Digital Production Services is a group within the Digital Library Services Division of the University of Texas at Austin Libraries
  • Concentrate on building digital collections and ensuring their long-term viability
  • The Divisions acts as a central services unit and provides services to the libraries, archives and museums on the University of Texas campus

How we got where we are..
  • Developed out of the need to reformat material from the archives and special collections held by the University of Texas
  • Focus was on high interest especially unique material (often rare)
  • Took the scan once approach
    • get as much detail as possible and be as careful as possible
  • Still with a focus on efficiency
8
Where we will be going
  • However, the upswing in interest in mass digitization efforts has worked to change that focus…
  • The expectations have reduced the time available to spend on the material
  • Will require that we concentrate on more general, less rare material
  • Library Participation in large resource sharing efforts such as the Texas Digital Library Project and the Open Content Alliance
9
However…
  • we still have a need to work with the rare, high value material
  • The Library is also participating in the Texas Heritage Digitization Initiative, and has ongoing commitments to doing work for various collections and departments
  • How do we do both?  Can we?
10
How we plan to get there
  • Increased reliance on outsourcing for less fragile material
  • Increased focus on efficiency and workflows
  • Local parallel batching
  • Increased Process Automation
    • Testing image work flow management tool
  • Development of more flexible imaging systems
  • Exploration of automated OCR and structural markup
11
 But we won’t be alone
  • Increased workflow in Preservation
  • Increased workflow in Cataloging/Metadata Services
  • Demands on the Bibliographers and collection managers
  • Different kind of problem being passed to our programming and web development teams
12
How the library is responding
  • In order to address these issues, the library has formed an overarching team to work out the issues associated with managing the various digitization queues and the digital projects.
  • First focus will be to select and send out a set of material from the Benson Latin American Collection
  • The project will be used to test the processes we are developing to handle the communication amongst the various units

Naming Schemes
  • Small scale:
  • Project name àby descriptive full name (of collection, book)
  • File names à by descriptive full name (of item, image)
  • Folder names à not always consistent


  • Large scale:
  • Project id à unit acronym-batch#####-yyyy-mm-dd
  •                           blac-batch00005-2006-04-12
  • File name à holding agency-item identifier-######-image/page###
  •                            txu-oclc-1487960-350
  • Folder names à archival, publication, documentation
15
Workflows

  • Small scale:
  • Linear process
  • One or two projects at a time
  • Item by item, high level treatment (imaging/OCR/encoding)



  • Large scale:
  • Structured, streamlined, parallel processes
  • Numerous parallel projects at the same time
  • Batch processing – less quality, more efficiency à
  • Low quality is acceptable, sampled QC, less time-consuming treatment (imaging/OCR/encoding)
