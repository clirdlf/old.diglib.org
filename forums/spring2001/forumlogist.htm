Registration Information

Eligibility: The DLF Forum is open to staff at DLF member institutions. We have reserved 125 spaces for Forum attendees. Although we do not wish to impose restrictions on the number of participants from any single DLF member, institutions wishing to send more than 4 staff members should contact the DLF (dlf_forum@clir.org) in advance.

Deadline for Registration: April 13, 2001.
Eligible participants should send their names, titles, and institutional affiliations to dlf_forum@clir.org indicating the dates they plan to attend the Forum.

Cost: Participants are expected to cover the costs of their travel, room and board. No registration fee will apply and participants will be invited to join us for breakfast and lunch on Saturday, for a reception Saturday evening, and for breakfast on Sunday.

Guest Accommodations

A block of rooms has been set aside at the Hyatt Fisherman's Wharf Hotel. Rooms can be booked at the DLF Forum rate of $195 provided reservations are made with the Hyatt before April 16, 2001 and dependant on availability. Be sure to mention the DLF Forum when making your reservations.

Proposals for papers and panel discussions

Staff at DLF member institutions are invited to submit proposals for papers and panel discussions dealing with all aspects of digital library development, for example, architectural and technical issues; digital collections; digital preservation; digital library standards and practices; and organizational, policy, and business issues.

Particular emphasis may be given to papers and panel discussions that address issues having to do with digital library use, users, and user support. Under this heading, we would particularly welcome contributions dealing with:

  • methods and strategies for assessing users needs and/or for incorporating user needs assessment in the design and implementation of digital libraries and their online collections and services;
  • the development and support new users and new uses;
  • identifying and meeting the needs of distance users; and
  • online reference and other innovative user services.

Proposals for presentations should be brief (c.250 words) and supply a title and short abstract. Proposals for panel discussions may be slightly longer. They should supply a title for the panel, a brief description of its purpose and a list of participants' names. Where appropriate, they may also supply a title and a brief description of individual panelists' presentations.

Proposals should be submitted by email to dlf_forum@clir.org. Please include the phrase "forum proposal" as the subject heading in your email.

Proposals must be received by 30 March 2001 in order to ensure consideration.

All conference rooms will be equipped with standard audio facilities, an overhead projector, a PC, and an LCD overhead display.
