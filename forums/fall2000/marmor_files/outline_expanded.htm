"A note on sustainability"
A note on sustainability
DLF seeks to incubate digital library initiatives, not to own and operate them them in perpetuity (it’s not a McD franchise)
Sustainability is a more secure strategy than digital “life support” (whether in the form of speculative ongoing grant funding, dependence upon ongoing institutional and consortial investment, or ongoing government subsidy)
Sustainability requires the creation of value and holds us to a high standard

"Digital libraries that aim to..."
Digital libraries that aim to be sustainable should be:
developed strategically, that is
designed to meet essential and abiding needs in well-defined target audiences
informed by “market” research into user practices and needs (just ask Carole Palmer!)
able to “just say no” to candidate collections that are out of strategic scope

Thesis #2

"There is a fundamental tension..."
There is a fundamental tension between the strategic and the consortial (“supply-side”) approach to digital library development.  Consortial initiatives are likely to be shaped fundamentally by the interests, imperatives, and constraints of contributing institutions--rather than taking the practices and needs of users and potential users as their point of departure.  This way lies “uncritical mass” --at best an indirect route to sustainability.

"Thesis #3"
Thesis #3
Somebody oughta try making a “demand-side” digital library

"Digital libraries can be designed..."
Digital libraries can be designed for sustainability, but this requires adopting a strategic approach that takes user needs, not supply-side interests, imperatives and constraints, as the point of departure.  The DLF’s Academic Image Coop (AIC) is an attempt to create such a digital library.

"2 AIC goals"
 2 AIC goals
The AIC is developing a scaleable, and hopefully sustainable, image database that:
is designed to respond strategically to basic and abiding needs of a broad art historical marketplace
will evolve in accordance with a business plan that seeks to ensure sustainability by providing for the development of a suite of collections and services mapping directly to a series of targeted markets in the art history and visual resources fields
