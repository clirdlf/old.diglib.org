CDL: Mission
Founded 1997 by UC President Richard Atkinson,  "a library without walls.”
Charged with selection, building, management, and preservation of the University's shared digital resources and
 Applying new technologies to enhance sharing of the University's physical collections
A Co-Library -  the 10th “digital” library in 9 Campus system

CDL Model:
Collaborative Leadership
Select, design, build, manage, and preserve high-quality digital content
Identify and implement on-line tools and services for sharing, manipulating, and integrating scholarly content
Implement shared architectures to achieve digital resource interoperability and integration
Promote scholarly communication in a networked environment

OAC Background: Phases of Growth
1993 - 1995: Encoded Archival Description (EAD) developed at UC Berkeley BFAP
1995 - 1997: UC EAD Project established to create prototype union database of encoded archival finding aids
1997 - 1999: Successful UC-EAD Project becomes Online Archive of California (OAC)
1999 - 2001: OAC virtual collection development & creation of eScholarship resources
