5.  The USGS node of the National Geospatial Data Clearinghouse is a component of the National Spatial Data Infrastructure (NSDI). The Clearinghouse provides a pathway to find information about geospatial or spatially referenced data available from USGS. The information is in the form of metadata which describe the content, quality, condition, and other characteristics of data. This site is maintained by the USGS in cooperation with the U.S. Federal Geographic Data Committee (FGDC). The Geospatial Data Clearinghouse is a collection of over 250 spatial data servers, that have digital geographic data primarily for use in Geographic Information Systems (GIS), image processing systems, and other modeling software.

To recap I would like to cover the process from beginning to end.  While I do the overview, we can stop at each individual system and look at it’s respective metadata schema. 
The impetus for us to begin such a project was to first, increase access to our Geospatial Information Repository, CUGIR. 
1.CUGIR is our online system of geospatial data, CSDGM and geospatial data
2.CUGIR is part of the NSDI National Spatial Digital Infrastructure which consists of the CSDGM metadata
We ran a scripts that converts the CSDGM to MARC.
3.We wanted to use existing tools, so we decided to use CORC in the next phase.  OCLC’s ORC is the Cooperative Online Resource Catalog metadata creation system for bibliographic records describing electronic resources.  In CORC we did the authority control on the records, standardizing subject headings and adding a classification number.
4.From CORCE we could manipulate 2 different metadata schemes:  MARC and DC-RDF. 
5.We took the DC-RDF and stored it in a database for OAI and we took the MARC and loaded it into WorldCat and our online Public Access Catalog.
6.Witin all records lies the pointer to metadata surrogate, the bucket, which leads the user back to CUGIR,to complete the cycle. 
7.The beauty of the bucket is that it encapsulates the URL which remains stable and constant no matter what system it resides in. 
8.If the bibliographic data changes, then the bucket should be changed once even though 4 systems link to that bucket.
9.If the URL changes, then we only need to change the bucket, not the bibliographic records floating around in the OPAC, WorldCat, OAI, CUGIR,
