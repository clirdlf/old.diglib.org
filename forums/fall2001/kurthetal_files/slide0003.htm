    ~ Cornell University Library ~
The digital collection environment we’re seeking to bring to our users is multiply heterogeneous.
Its subject content has the potential to span all areas in which the library collects.
Central to our discussion today, the granularity of the objects targeted by system records ranges from one page of a digitized pamphlet to the search interface for Early English Books Online, which in turn opens onto over 100,000 digitized books and serials.
The native metadata sets we’re importing vary as well.  So far we’ve been working with MARC, TEILite, EAD, DC, and locally developed element sets.
By saying the environment is heterogeneous in terms of penetration, I mean that we are variously able to provide direct access to the objects described by the metadata we’re importing.  In the case of MARC records describing physical artifacts in the CUL collection, the penetration begins and ends with the metadata itself.  EAD finding aids for undigitized collections go farther in representing content, as do records linked to scanned images of three-dimensional objects, for example.  And, records capable of delivering born-digital texts or images go farther still.
Finally, the format of represented objects varies to the extent offered by past and present technologies.
An important outcome of looking at the collection environment in this way is to recognize that each of these facets intersects with the others to a greater or lesser extent.  Depth of penetration as we’ve seen is closely linked to resource description and format.  Similarly, we can’t address aspects of granularity without considering how they interact with other environmental factors.
