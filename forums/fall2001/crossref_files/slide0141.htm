Is it working?
•88 member publishers, and 20 library or other affiliates
•Over 3.7 million articles registered
•More than 5,000 journals in the system
•More than 80% of the publishers are currently depositing and 30% linking
•968,576 DOI resolutions in August/ 800,000 in October
•2 million articles per year to be added
•Conference proceedings and books in 2002
YES!
So the main question is – is CrossRef working?  The answer is a resounding yes.  There are 83 member publishers and 20 libraries and affiliates.  Over 3.5 million articles have been registered from over 5,000 journals.  More than 80% of the publishers are depositing and a growing are linking.

More importantly and increasing number of CrossRef DOIs are in journal article references and users are clicking them.  The DOI Foundation provides statistics on the number of DOI resolutions and there were almost 1 million in July and 8,000,000 in September.  Some of this is increased usage, but a lot of it is also testing – for instance 3,000,000 resolutions of one DOI occurred in one day.  This level of resolutions really put the DOI system through its paces and it performed very well.

CrossRef is on track to registered between 500,000 and 1 million articles per year.
