What is CrossRef
An independent, non-profit membership association
A cross-publisher citation linking network based on DOIs
A DOI Registration Agency
Mission: to be the complete citation linking backbone for all scholarly literature in electronic form
Things CrossRef is NOT:
A software product for sale
A direct-to-end-user service
A search interface
A broker of full-text content
Made up of just big commercial publishers
Intended to make library users pay for articles they already have  subscription rights to

What do publishers need to do?
For INBOUND links.publishers deposit metadata for online publications with CrossRef, including a DOI and URL
For OUTBOUND links.publishers send references to CrossRef database to find the DOI for the cited content and use the DOI to make a link in their content
Two key benefits of the CrossRef/DOI system
A DOI link is a persistent link, unlike a URL
No stale links in citations or database records
Gives publishers flexibility to update URLs as needed
CrossRef so far has seen about 40% of URLs in its database change
No tracking of publisher linking schemes needed
A single agreement with CrossRef serves as a linking agreement with all participating publishers
No bilateral linking agreements needed

Expansion of content types
What: Migrating from a limited, proprietary DTD for journal citations only to a unified ONIX-compatible XML schema for journals, conference proceedings, and reference works.
What for: (1) To enable citation linking into and out of other content types. (2) Will facilitate access to online proceedings papers and book chapters.
Multiple resolution
What: A single DOI associated with many possible actions
What for: Could include (1) multiple URLs for dispersed mirror sites, (2) pointer to a metadata record, (3) access to sub-parts of an article, such as abstract or references only, (4) email address, (5) different versions (get html or get pdf), (5) author bio, (6) rights information, etc
Multiple Resolution for Journals and Conference Proceedings
CrossSearch
What: Distributed cross-publisher search of full-text content.
What for: Offer a new way for researchers to navigate invisible web, proprietary, peer-reviewed material, assuring both breadth of search and authoritative search results.
