Registration Information

Eligibility: The DLF Forum is open to staff at DLF member institutions. Although we do not wish to impose restrictions on the number of participants from any single DLF member, institutions wishing to send more than 4 staff members should contact the DLF (dlf_forum@clir.org) in advance.

Deadline for Registration: April 22, 2002.
Eligible participants should either

  • fill out the DLF Forum registration form or
  • send their names, titles, and institutional affiliations to dlf_forum@clir.org indicating the dates they plan to attend the Forum.

Cost: Participants are expected to cover the costs of their travel, room and board. No registration fee will apply and participants will be invited to join us for a reception Friday evening, for breakfast and lunch on Saturday, and for breakfast on Sunday. In addition, there will be numerous breaks at which snacks and beverages will be provided.

Guest Accommodations

A block of rooms has been set aside at the Wyndham Chicago. Rooms can be booked at the DLF Forum rate of $149 per night provided reservations are made with the Wyndham before April 8, 2002 and depending on availability. Be sure to mention the DLF Forum when making your reservations.

Proposals for papers and panel discussions

Staff at DLF member institutions are invited to submit proposals for papers and panel discussions by dealing with any aspect of digital library development.

At this forum, there will be a special emphasis on interoperability - what it is, why it is significant for research libraries and their patrons, and how it may be achieved. The topic will be approached from all its many technological, organizational, definitional, political and other aspects.

Proposals for presentations should be brief (c.250 words) and supply a title and short abstract.

Proposals for panel discussions may be slightly longer. They should supply a title for the panel discussion, briefly outline its purpose, and supply the panelists' names alongside titles and short descriptions for their respective contributions.

Proposals should be submitted by email to dlf_forum@clir.org. Please include the phrase "forum proposal" as the subject heading in your email.

Proposals must be received by March 22, 2002 in order to ensure consideration.

All conference rooms will be equipped with standard audio facilities and an LCD overhead display.

Host or participate in a "birds-of-a-feather" discussion

As a new departure, the DLF Forum will accommodate a number of "birds of a feather" discussions on topics that may be recommended by participants. Such discussions may be used to foster inter-institutional collaborations, "cook up" new DLF initiatives, make progress with DLF initiatives already underway, or simply share experiences in some area. Discussions will take place at tables reserved at breakfast on Saturday and Sunday and at Saturday lunch.

Those interested in hosting such discussions are asked to send an email to dlf_forum@clir.org by April 22, 2002 suggesting topics. Birds-of-a-feather discussions will be advertised in advance of the forum.

If you have any questions at all about the forum, please contact Cassie Savage.
