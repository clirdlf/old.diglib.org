DRAFT TEXT OF LETTER TO CROSSREF

Prepared by Dale Flecker, 2 Feb 2000
Edited for presentation to the DLF Steering Committee by D Greenstein, 5 Mar 2000

The undersigned are a group of research libraries actively engaged in acquiring and delivering electronic journals to their constituencies. We have read with considerable interest and excitement the announcements concerning CrossRef, which we believe to be a major development in this arena, with significant potential for increasing the utility of e-journals. The ability to navigate easily between related articles, tracing the “web” of thought and research on a topic, is something widely desired by scholars and is of obvious great utility.

We are writing, however, to express one concern we have about the current technical underpinnings of CrossRef. We understand that CrossRef will use the DOI and PubRef identifiers and resolution systems to provide address look-up for linking purposes. The PubRef system provides a limited but useful mechanism to support selective linkage to more than one copy of an article when such exists. It is our understanding that at this point the DOI, the primary pointing mechanism CrossRef will use, does not have such a facility. As it is currently being implemented, the DOI will give access only to the copy of an article mounted by the publisher doing the original registration. If an institution has an alternate location from which its users should get access to an article, this alternate copy will not be found.

In the current environment there are two major sources of alternative copies for e-journal articles: locally loaded copies, and materials provided by “aggregator” services (OCLC, EBSCO, IAC, Bell and Howell, Ovid, etc.). In addition to these two current types of sources for alternate copies, there are at least two other areas receiving considerable attention today that are likely to introduce yet more complexity into the question of finding an “appropriate copy” of an article for users: e-print services and archiving initiatives. A number of publishers are now cooperating with organized e-print services (Cornell, for instance, is actively developing plans for e- print services in specific STM areas), and these will represent alternative sources for some journal articles. The research library community has been active engaged in discussions with publishers in recent months about the need for independent archival storage of e-journals to insure long-term availability and preservation. It is highly likely that these discussions will lead to initiatives which will create yet more alternate copies of articles, copies which over time will need to be encompassed in any citation linking infrastructure.

We are writing today for a couple of purposes. First, while we know that this issue is one CrossRef recognizes, we would like to emphasize its importance to us, and we believe to a number of CrossRef publishers. The publishers of journals (along with the editors of journals and the authors of articles) have a great interest in promoting the widest availability of their publications, regardless of the location of the copy accessed (assuming, of course, the copy is accessed under the appropriate license). Making reference linking work across all legal copies of articles serves the best interests of scholars, libraries, and publishers. Second, we recognize that the issue of “appropriate copy” linking is technically complex (several of the signers have written papers on possible technical approaches), and that considerable thought and discussion will be required to identify appropriate solutions. We would like to volunteer to participate in any way appropriate in such discussions, and to serve as beta sites for testing possible solutions.

Reference linking is an exciting development, providing functionality and convenience impossible in the paper journal world. Like most useful technologies it presents technical challenges if it is to be addressed in the most general and powerful manner. We heartily applaud the CrosssRef initiative, and hope that we can help with developing reference linking in a way that will best serve all of the players in the e-journal environment!
