Agenda for a Meeting of the Digital Library Federation Steering Committee, to be held on 17 March 2000

The meeting will take place from 10am - 4pm in the Stein Room of the Brookings Institute, 1775 Massachusetts Avenue, NW, Washington, DC. Papers for the meeting are linked to this agenda. Members are asked to print and bring their own copies of the paper to the meeting.

Discussion at the meeting will determine the DLF's direction over the next few years. Although every effort will be made to conclude the meeting expeditiously, members are requested to plan on a full 6-hour session (punctuated by lunch) if at all possible.

Agenda

  1. Minutes of the last meeting

    The minutes of the meeting held on 24 Sep 99 are available for information

  2. Matters arising
  3. DLF business plan

    Discussion of the business plan is the key item on the agenda for the meeting. The plan is a detailed document that suggests a road map for the DLF. It outlines possible program areas, addresses a range of key organizational, operational and resource issues, and recommends a number of next steps. The plan will be circulated electronically and be available well in advance of the meeting. At the meeting, members are invited to consider the plan and to discuss and agree next steps as follows:

    1. Brief presentation highlighting key strategic issues that require the directors' attention (DG)

    2. Small breakout groups convened to consider the plan and the strategic issues arising from it

      Break for Lunch

    3. Breakout groups report back on their discussions to the committee re-convened as a whole and general discussion

    4. Wrap up, summary, and discussion of next steps
  4. Report from the DLF Secretariat

    The report summarizes progress in areas where the DLF is currently active and highlight significant developments since the last meeting of the Steering Committee.
