DLF Steering Committee

Minutes of meeting on September 24, 1999

Present: Scott Bennett (Yale), Rosann Bazirjian (for Nancy Eaton, Penn State), L. Reynolds Cahoon (NARA), Dale Flecker (for Nancy Cline, Harvard), Joan Gotwals (Emory), Rebecca Graham (DLF), Bernie Hurley (for Gerald Lowell, UC Berkeley), Paula Kaufman (Tennessee), Wendy Lougee (for Bill Gosling, Michigan), Clifford Lynch (CNI), Deanna Marcum (CLIR), Charlene Mason, (for Tom Shaughnessy, Minnesota), Mark McFarland (for Harold Billings, University of Texas), James Michalko (RLG), Paul Mosher (Penn), Donald Muccino (OCLC), Judith Nadler (for Martin Runkle, Chicago), John Ober (for Richard Lucier, CDL), Assunta Pisani (for Michael Keller, Stanford), Phil Reese (for Jerry Campbell USC), Elaine Sloan (Columbia), Winston Tabb (LC), Sarah Thomas (Cornell), Suzanne Thorin (Indiana), Denise Troll, ( for Gloriana St. Clair, Carnegie Mellon), William Walker (NYPL), Donald Waters (DLF),

Unable to attend: Aubrey Mitchell (Tennessee), Susan Nutter (NC State), Karin Trainer (Princeton)

Sarah Thomas, chair, opened the meeting, introductions were made and a welcome was extended to the University of Virginia as the newest DLF member.

Rebecca Graham reviewed highlights of DLF activities since the last Steering Committee meeting. Jim Michalko reported that work of the taskforces is continuing and there will be a report early next year.

Dale Flecker and Priscilla Caplan have drafted and are circulating for comment a paper on the "appropriate copy" aspect of reference linking http://www.lib.uchicago.edu/Annex/pcaplan/reflink.html .

The next DLF Forum, addressing organizational and policy issues related to digital library initiatives, will be held at Emory University on March 31 - April 2, 2000.

Cliff Lynch reported on the PKI & security workshop sponsored by Educause in August. The proceedings from the workshop are available at http://www.educause.edu/netatedu/contents/events/aug99/index.html

DLF director designee, Dan Greenstein's presentation Libraries in a Networked Digital Age: A Framework for Federation ( http://www.diglib.org/presentations/sc999/sld001.htm) was followed by a discussion among members on a broad range of topics identified during Dan's presentation. Dan plans to visit all DLF member institutions in the early days of the new year to inform the business plan he has committed to delivering in advance of the next meeting which is scheduled for Friday, March 17, 2000.

The final speaker was Walt Warnick, Director of the Office of Scientific and Technical Information at the Department of Energy. Dr. Warnick presented The Foundation of a Digital Library in the Physical Sciences which furnished background information on the PubSCIENCE system which will provide access to many of the scientific and journal literature in the physical sciences ( http://pubsci.osti.gov/).

The meeting adjourned at 2:45 pm.
