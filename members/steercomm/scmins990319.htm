DLF Steering Committee

Minutes of meeting on March 19, 1999

Present: Scott Bennett (Yale), Harold Billings (University of Texas), Liz Bishoff (OCLC, for Donald Muccino), L. Reynolds Cahoon (NARA), Priscilla Caplan (for Martin Runkle, Chicago), Nancy Cline (Harvard), Nancy Eaton (Penn State), Bill Gosling (Michigan), Rebecca Graham (DLF), Joan Gotwals (Emory), Bernie Hurley (for Jerry Lowell, UC Berkeley), Paula Kaufman (Tennessee), Heike Kordish (for William Walker, NYPL), Richard Lucier (CDL), Deanna Marcum (CLIR), James Michalko (RLG), Paul Mosher (Penn), Susan Nutter (NC State), Gloriana St. Clair (Carnegie Mellon), Tom Shaughnessy (Minnesota), Elaine Sloan (Columbia), Winston Tabb (LC), Sarah Thomas (Cornell), Suzanne Thorin (Indiana), Catherine Tierney (for Michael Keller, Stanford), Donald Waters (DLF)

Unable to attend: Jerry Campbell (USC), Karin Trainer (Princeton)

Guest: Dr. Elmar Mittler, Director of the Niedersaechsische Staats und Universitaetsbibliothek, Goettigen

Paula Kaufman, chair, opened the meeting at 10:00 a.m. She welcomed Harold Billings of the University of Texas at Austin as the newest Federation member.

The minutes of the last meeting were approved after Don Waters explained that the earlier draft had been revised upon hearing member reactions to the section on digital archiving.

Review of DLF Activities. Members of the Steering Committee applauded Waters and Graham for the level of DLF activity in recent months and the many accomplishments. Waters briefly reviewed the full activity report and responded to members' questions and comments.

Members of the Committee agreed that more frequent communication is needed the DLF Director and partners. Nancy Eaton urged that DLF staff evaluate developments to help directors understand where their influence is needed, particularly so that they can help ensure the creation of interoperable rather than competing systems. One area in which such advice is needed is in pointing out how DLF certification and authentication activities differ from those of the CIC.

The members of the Committee also discussed ways to improve the communication and interactions among the DLF partners. Priscilla Caplan and others emphasized the need to get the technical staffs together to share ideas and approaches to problems.

Budget Analysis. The members engaged in a discussion of how best to use the capital funds that have been accumulating. Several members of the committee emphasized that the amount of money is not large compared to the money that ultimately will need to be spent. Elaine Sloan noted that many of the projects now underway have projected publications and follow-on work attached to them that will require expenditure of these funds.

Future activities. After reviewing the documents on future activities that Waters distributed, the Steering Committee agreed with the general divisions of work. Members agreed with the emphasis on supporting digital library activities that advanced learning, research and publication, but questioned whether the characterization of these as "discipline-based" activities is the most effective description. The committee decided that the proposed clearinghouse of DLF digitizing projects is not worth the investment and should not be pursued. Effort should be spent instead on finding effective ways to bring partners together to collaborate on projects. The members adopted the plan identified for an annual review of alliances. And the proposed budget for the coming year was approved.

DLF and international work on digital libraries. Over lunch, Dr. Mittler gave a provocative talk suggesting numerous ways that the DLF might facilitate work between the DLF partners and institutions abroad. This is a topic to which the Steering Committee will return in a future meeting.

Digital Archiving. Jim Michalko presented the findings of the Hedstrom study that RLG recently commissioned. In the subsequent discussion, DLF members determined that the interests of research libraries in digital archiving should be identified and documented not through the formulation of policies, but by recording and analyzing the practices currently in place at DLF institutions for various classes of materials. As several members phrased it, we want to know what current practices are so we can decide what needs to be done. DLF will work with RLG to form a group to document these current practices.

Waters observed that both libraries and publishers have increasingly recognized that long-term maintenance of the published literature in digital form is a critical business issue that must be addressed. It may also be a business opportunity. The Steering Committee asked the DLF staff to document existing economic and organizational models for digital archiving and then to form a DLF group to work with publishers and others and devise some practical options for the long-term maintenance of digital publications.

Report from Newer Members. The members of the Steering Committee found the written reports from the new members to be very interesting and informative. They concluded that a portion of the membership should report in a similar way at each meeting.

At 2:45 p.m., the Steering Committee adjourned. The next meeting is scheduled for June 18, 1999.
