DL developments reflected in our R&D
Driving down the costs associated with members’ maturation by:
documenting existing practical experience and synthesizing lessons learned from it (collection strategies, use assessment methods, organizational models and issues)
identifying good or preferred practice in some of that experience (text encoding, image production and quality assessment, preservation reformatting, technical metadata, visual resource description,
EAD?)
leveraging our shared understandings to influence the behavior of information suppliers (Liblicense, open linking)
moving quickly to address new challenges as and when we discover them (learning about libraries as part of an information landscape)
DL developments reflected in our catalytic initiatives
Supporting emphasis on infrastructure over experimentation:
instigating, fostering, or attracting investment in core technologies and protocols (open linking, metadata harvesting, authentication?)
articulating requirements of, aggregating demand for, and attracting investment in services that are commonly required but beyond any institution’s ability to supply (ArtSTOR, archival repositories, harvesting services, registry services?, digitization centers?)
facilitating collaboration around infrastructure, not shared collections or “killer apps”
