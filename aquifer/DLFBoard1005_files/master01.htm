Brief overview of the initiative for those new to DLF, progress to date, dreams for the future. Some of this will be redundant to those libraries that have recently had site visits and certainly to Martin, Martha, Mike, Winston and David who are directly involved.
Initiative is open for others to join. Kickoff requirement was to contribute staff to at least two working groups. Working groups are now well formed so other way to participate now in development. May include hosting a particular service or development segment.
By pooling and piping we mean aggregating or taking from current context and enabling objects to be combined with others in a new context. The vision is to enable this re-contextualizing infinitely.
Not a digital library workflow.
Overlap between participants in “feeder” activities. Aquifer making good use of OAI best practices development, data provider training, services development based on information from scholars. Quote comes from Lorcan Dempsey’s weblog. Is a better way of saying “wouldn’t it be nice to support the infinite decontextualizing and recontextualizing of content?”
How interested are people in this?
Landing gear is down but we haven’t quite made contact with the runway.
We are now working on some very nitty-gritty issues.
Metadata intern from San Jose State University is using the draft MODS profile to evaluate the metadata for the test collections to see how closely the profile is met, what mapping might need to be done, etc. Getting set up for metadata harvesting.
We don’t currently have a way to harvest selectively from collections. Will we develop this ability as an Aquifer service?
Indiana University was preparing this collection to be MODS harvest-able and used the DLF Aquifer project as a focus for completing the work. This is an example of a collection that has selected material in it pertaining to Abraham Lincoln and the Civil War.
While this collection at University of Michigan contains fictional material about the Civil War period, as a fiction collection, it does not include subject headings so machine filtering becomes an interesting problem.
Another collection from which selected material would be appropriate for inclusion. In this case, machine selection based on subject headings would be feasible.
This collection from LC is clearly appropriate for inclusion in its entirety.
As it this collection at University of Michigan.
On the cusp of selecting a participant library to do harvesting
Scope of work this group is doing is large and overarching. Services means both end user tools and machine-to-machine functionality.
Group is doing a good job of taking cues from services—holding back from “cool tools” development.
Findings from second generation meeting David will report on—scholars don’t care where the material is located and may not care about local context. Not another portal! The library as hosted social space for scholarship.
Participant libraries are developing tools locally that meet some recognized needs. We have begun registering these tools to evaluate them for incorporation into DLF Aquifer.
Resources for the initiative come from the participant libraries. We’ll look in a little more detail at how this is currently structured when we review the budget proposal. Some people think this initiative is too complex to carry out without a more robust central organization or a host library. I suggest that keeping the initiative as distributed as possible will allow us to demonstrate the power of the network. We don’t have to centralize to offer massive collections or to be “hub” facilitators. We talk about transforming the way we work. One aspect of this transformation is to find ways to prioritize work within each institution so resources for collaboration can be realized. How do we identify capacity and capture it? How do we insure that we are always thinking about how what we are doing might contribute more broadly?
Thanks to David’s careful stewardship, much has come out of DLF operating (travel, meeting support). Library in-kind contributions to the planning effort and to support staff attending meetings is already significant. Budget calculations show 27K per library across the board. Although we have an active proposal with the Hewlett Foundation and several other strong possibilities for future funding cycles, I don’t expect a significant influx of outside funds until FY07. Thus, the projected budget will include a request for additional DLF capital funds to support the initiative in the coming year.
