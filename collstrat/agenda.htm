The meeting will be convened at 2pm on 15 January 2001 in the Clifton Ford Room of the Jury's Doyle Hotel, 1500 New Hampshire Avenue, NW. Washington DC

Directions. By Metro to Dupont Circle. The hotel is on New Hampshire just north of the Circle.

1. Welcome and introduction (DG)

2. Review of drafts with reference to initiative's aims and objectives

Here we will review the drafts to see how far they go in meeting initial aims and objectives as set out in the framing document Developing Sustainable, Scaleable, Digital Library Collections...(August 2000). Amongst the questions we will want to address are the following:

  • How far do the drafts go in addressing themselves to the problem that is stated in this framing document?

  • How far do the drafts go in meeting the stated aims?

  • How and to what extent do they address themselves to the intended audience(s)?

  • How well do they conform to the intended outline?

  • What good practice recommendations can we or should we expect from the reports? At what level and with what detail should they be made?

  • How much in the way of bibliography and pointers should we expect from the drafts?

With each of these questions we will want to ask where and how the drafts AND/OR the initial problem statement need revision.

3. Review of the drafts

Although members of the group are welcome to comment on any aspect of the drafts, we will want to cover the questions the authors themselves have raised. These questions are explicitly documented in the drafts themselves. It would be helpful if those responsible for a detailed reading of a draft could lead off discussion as follows:

4. Next steps

Again with reference to the framing document, we want to discuss the drafts' further development and publication, but also how far we can go (whether in these drafts or through some follow up work) in identifying, and developing consensus around "good" collection development practices, and what process that work might involve.
